Privacy

This is the privacy statement of Thrive People B.V. (hereinafter "Thrive," "we," "us" or "our"). In this statement we explain how we collect and process personal data, why we do this, what data we collect and what rights you have in relation to your personal data. 1. Who are we Thrive People B.V. focuses on the vitality and well-being of employers and their employees. We act in accordance with applicable privacy legislation and ensure that your personal data is processed securely. 2. Collection and use of personal data We collect personal data when you contact us or use our services. This data may include your name, contact information and other relevant information. We use your personal data for the following purposes: • Communicating with you about our services and offers . • Improving our services and website. • Providing our services and managing your account. • Complying with legal obligations. 3. Purpose of processing personal data Our goal is to serve website and platform visitors and provide them with valuable information and tools. The content of our online environment focuses on promoting vitality and well-being. To improve our services and content and to provide relevant content, we process personal data of our website and platform visitors. By analyzing interactions we can adjust and optimize our website and platform, allowing us to offer a better user experience. 4. Matomo We use Matomo on our website to gain insight into the behavior of our visitors and to improve the performance of our website and platform. Matomo Analytics collects anonymous information about your interaction with our website and platform, such as your location, browser type and the pages visited. Your IP address is anonymized to protect your privacy. You can manage the collection of this data via our consent bar. For more information about Matoma's privacy policy, please refer to the Matom0 website (https://matomo.org/). 5. What personal data do we process We collect personal data when you contact us, use our service extension and when you provide information to us yourself. As part of our services, we also receive personal data from our customers that we may process as a processor. 6. Legal bases for data processing In accordance with applicable privacy legislation, Thrive People B.V. processes. data from its website and platform visitors. • Legitimate interest: Thrive People has a legitimate interest in processing the data to improve services and offer the Thrive Platform to employees. The following legal bases enable Thrive People to provide employees with access to the Thrive Platform at the request of their employer, allowing employees to make appointments with service providers and receive relevant services. With each data processing, the applicable conditions are presented to employees to protect their rights and privacy. In addition, Thrive People B.V. processes business email addresses and private mobile telephone numbers of employees of its customers on the basis of the following legal bases: • Performance of a contract: The processing is necessary for the execution of the agreement between Thrive People and the employer, where Thrive People offers access to the Thrive Platform. Users of the Thrive Platform are also asked to share information about their vitality so that the range of providers and products meets the needs of the user. The following basis applies: • Consent (if applicable): In some cases, processing may take place on the basis of consent given by the data subjects, such as employees. 7. Security of personal data We take appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, loss or misuse. We do this in accordance with the applicable legal requirements and guidelines. 8. Retention period of personal data Personal data will be retained for as long as is necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, unless legal regulations require otherwise. 9. Sharing personal data with third parties We only share your personal data with third parties in the following situations: • With your consent. • With our trusted partners and service providers to provide our services. • To comply to legal obligations. • In connection with business transactions, such as mergers, acquisitions or sales of assets. 10. Your rights You have certain privacy rights, including the right to access, correct, erase, transfer or restrict processing of your personal data. You can object to the processing of your data at any time. If you have any questions about your privacy rights or our data processing, please contact us. 11. More information For more information about our data processing and the situations in which personal data is processed, please contact us.
